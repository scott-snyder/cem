One of the most distinguished members of the party of thirty Chinese
students who have just arrived in this country to be educated is Ngua
Lauye. He begins the study of English after having won the highest
academic honors conferred at Pekin. He is a Cantonese, and the son of
a distinguished magistrate. At the age of twenty he was the successful
competitor at the provincial examination for master of arts.  At
twenty-four he obtained, at Pekin, the Han Lin degree, somewhat
similar to the English LL.D. He was employed as a secretary in one of
the public departments at Pekin when the Tsung-Li-Yamen nominated
him. with eight others, to the Throne, as fit to be intrusted with
carrying out the new programme of imperial improvement, the
publication of which attracted considerable attention recently. He is
now in his twenty-ninth year.
