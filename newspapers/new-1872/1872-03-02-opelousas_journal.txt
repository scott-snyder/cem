A very smart young Chinaman named Yung Wing graduated at Yale College
in 1854. He stood at the head of his class, and was especially
distinguished for his talents as a writer of the English language.  He
has now been employed by the Chinese government to bring over thirty
other young Chinamen, who are to be educated at Yale College at the
public expense. It is said that thirty Chinese students are to he
entered at Yale College yearly during the next ten years, and a sum of
$150,000 a year has been appropriated by the Chinese government to
pay their expenses.
